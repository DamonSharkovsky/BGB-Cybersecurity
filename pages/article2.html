Hi everyone! I'm not very tech-savvy but I've been getting some emails that seem suspiciously well-written for scammers. They have perfect grammar, sound very professional, and even include personal details about me that seem accurate.

The thing is, something still feels "off" about these emails. They're too polished, and the writing style feels somehow... artificial? I've heard that scammers are now using AI to write convincing emails, but how can someone like me tell the difference?

Examples of what I'm seeing:

  • Perfect grammar and spelling (unusual for typical scam emails)
  • Very formal, corporate language throughout
  • Long, detailed explanations that seem to go in circles
  • Generic personal touches that could apply to anyone

My specific concerns:

  1. How can I identify AI-written text in emails?
  2. What are the telltale signs I should look for?
  3. Are there any simple tools to help detect AI-generated content?
  4. Should I be more suspicious of "perfect" emails now?

Any advice would be greatly appreciated! I want to protect myself but don't want to become paranoid about every email I receive.

Great question! Here are the key signs to identify AI-generated emails:

üîç Language Patterns:

  • Repetitive phrasing or sentence structures
  • Overly formal tone that doesn't match the context
  • Generic statements that could apply to anyone
  • Lack of genuine personal touches or errors humans make

üö© Red Flags:

  • Perfect grammar but odd word choices
  • Information that's technically correct but feels "scraped"
  • Responses that don't quite address your specific situation
  • Inconsistent knowledge (knows some details but misses obvious ones)

üõ†Ô∏è Simple Detection Tips:

  • Ask specific questions only the real sender would know
  • Look for contextual understanding, not just information regurgitation
  • Check if the email addresses your exact situation or uses generic responses

@TechNewbie - You're absolutely right to trust your instincts! Here's a simple test I recommend:

"The Human Response Test":

  1. Reply with a specific, personal question
  2. Include an intentional minor error in your message
  3. Ask about something that requires real understanding, not just information lookup

AI responses often miss nuanced questions and won't address the human context of your situation. Real humans will often acknowledge your error or ask for clarification.
