üö® URGENT WARNING: I need to share this experience to protect other families from a sophisticated AI voice cloning scam that nearly fooled my tech-savvy grandmother.

What Happened:

Yesterday at 3 PM, my 78-year-old grandmother received a call from someone who sounded EXACTLY like me. The caller said:

"Grandma, it's me! I'm in trouble and need you to wire $2,000 immediately. I was in a car accident and I'm at the police station. Please don't tell my parents - I don't want them to worry. Can you help me?"

How They Did It:

The scammers had scraped audio from my Instagram stories and TikTok videos where I talk directly to the camera. They used AI voice cloning technology to replicate my voice perfectly, including my speech patterns and the way I call her "Grandma."

Red Flags That Saved Her:

  • I never call her just "Grandma" - I always say "Hi Grandma Rose"
  • The caller avoided answering when she asked about our last conversation
  • There was no background noise you'd expect from a police station
  • The urgency and secrecy request felt manipulative

How to Protect Your Family:

  1. Set up code words: Have secret phrases for emergency situations
  2. Always verify: Hang up and call the person's known number
  3. Ask personal questions: Details only the real person would know
  4. Trust your instincts: If something feels off, investigate
  5. Limit social media audio: Be careful about posting videos with clear audio

Please share this to protect other families! These scams are becoming incredibly sophisticated and targeting our most vulnerable family members.
