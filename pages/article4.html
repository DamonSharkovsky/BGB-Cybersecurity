What should I teach my grandchildren about AI safety?

#ChildSafety #AISafety #DigitalLiteracy #FamilyEducation

Hello everyone. I'm a 69-year-old grandfather of four wonderful grandchildren (ages 8, 12, 14, and 16). They're all very comfortable with technology - much more than I am! They use smartphones, play online games, chat with friends on various apps, and even help me with my computer problems.

However, after reading about all these new AI scams on this community, I'm becoming quite worried. My grandchildren are smart kids, but they might not recognize these new types of threats that use artificial intelligence. As someone who didn't grow up with this technology, I feel a bit lost about how to protect them.

My specific concerns:

  • Online interactions: How can they tell if they're chatting with a real person or an AI?
  • Voice and video calls: What if someone uses fake voices or videos to trick them?
  • Social media: How do they identify AI-generated content that might be misleading?
  • Gaming and apps: Are there AI-related risks in the platforms they use daily?
  • Academic work: How should they use AI tools responsibly for homework?

What I want to teach them:

  1. How to recognize when AI might be involved in their online interactions
  2. Red flags to watch out for in messages, calls, and videos
  3. When and how to verify if someone is really who they claim to be
  4. Safe practices for sharing personal information online
  5. How to handle suspicious situations and who to tell

The challenge:

I want to educate them without making them paranoid or afraid of technology. They should be able to enjoy the benefits of the internet while being smart about potential dangers. I also don't want to sound like I don't understand their world - I want to have productive conversations that they'll actually listen to.

Questions for the community:

  • What are the most important AI safety concepts for children and teens?
  • How can I explain these concepts in age-appropriate ways?
  • Are there any good resources, videos, or games that teach AI safety to young people?
  • What warning signs should I teach them to look for?
  • How can I keep these conversations ongoing without being overbearing?
  • Should I set up any specific rules or tools on their devices?

I love my grandchildren dearly and want to make sure they can navigate this AI-enhanced world safely. Any advice from parents, educators, tech experts, or even young people themselves would be greatly appreciated.

Thank you in advance for your help!

P.S. - If any young people are reading this and have suggestions for how their grandparents could talk to them about this topic, I'd love to hear your perspective too!

What a thoughtful question! Here's an age-appropriate breakdown:

üßí Ages 8-10: Basic Concepts

  • "Computers can pretend to be people" - simple but true
  • Never share passwords, even with "friends" online
  • Always ask a grown-up before clicking links or downloading anything
  • If something feels weird or too good to be true, tell an adult

üéÆ Ages 11-13: Intermediate Awareness

  • Explain that "bots" aren't just in games - they're everywhere online
  • Teach them to question perfect grammar in suspicious messages
  • Create a family code word for emergency situations
  • Show them how to verify friend requests through other channels

üéì Ages 14-16: Advanced Understanding

  • Discuss deepfakes and voice cloning technology
  • Teach critical thinking about online content
  • Explain the importance of digital footprints
  • Cover responsible AI use for schoolwork

I love that you're being proactive! Here's what worked with my teens:

"The STOP Method":

  • Stop - pause before responding to urgent requests
  • Think - does this make sense?
  • Other channel - verify through a different method
  • Parent/adult - ask for help if unsure

We practice this with real scenarios at dinner time!
